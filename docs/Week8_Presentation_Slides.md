## Presentation Notes & Tips

### **Timing Breakdown (10 minutes):**
- **Minute 1-2**: Problem and motivation (Slides 2-3)
- **Minute 3-4**: Literature review and innovation (Slide 4)
- **Minute 5-7**: Live demo and progress evidence (Slides 5-9)
- **Minute 8-10**: Future plans and A1 goals (Slides 10-14)

### **Key Messages to Emphasize:**
1. **"This isn't just another study app"** - it's the first to combine individual + social + AI
2. **"We have a working application"** - not just a prototype, with substantial code
3. **"Focus on real-world impact"** - user-centered design over technical complexity
4. **"Revolutionary approach"** - could change how students study

### **Demo Strategy:**
1. **Show the working app** - live demonstration
2. **Highlight unique features** - Study Together, real-time collaboration
3. **Demonstrate data persistence** - auto-save, cross-tab sync
4. **Show social aspects** - friends, messaging, leaderboard

### **Q&A Preparation:**
- Be ready to discuss technical decisions (React, Node.js, JSON storage)
- Explain your approach to user research and validation
- Discuss scalability and future development plans
- Show confidence in your chosen direction while being open to feedback

### **Visual Aids Suggestions:**
- **Screenshots**: Login, Study Session, Study Together, Friends, Leaderboard
- **Architecture Diagram**: Frontend/Backend relationship
- **User Flow**: Student journey through the app
- **Timeline**: 7-month development roadmap
- **Metrics**: Current progress and future goals
